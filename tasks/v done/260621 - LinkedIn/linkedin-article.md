# I made a system for self-improving companies

Most teams bolt AI onto existing workflow. We think the real shift is a different shape
for how work happens: a **closed loop** that gets better every time it runs.

**Workflow** → Some automation. Outcomes aren't fed back. Manual work.
**Closed loop** → The closed-loop self-improving system (the engine).

  capture → analyze → propose → improve, artifact-rich, gets better over time

A whole-company model

- Loops everywhere
- Queryable org
- Software factories
- Token-maxing
- New org archetypes
- No middle management

Every action → an artifact the AI learns from — the append-only store is "make it queryable / legible to AI".

Self-improves over time — next run ingests accumulated history.

## What makes it real (no demo)

- **Append-only audit log** — every decision + outcome, with reasoning. The audit trail *is* the memory.
- **A safety hard floor** — reversible actions run on their own; anything irreversible is blocked from auto-running *in code* and waits for a human.
- **Humans at the edge** — guiding and approving, don't route every message. Agent does the work.

We aren't adding AI to the company. We're rebuilding the company as a loop.
